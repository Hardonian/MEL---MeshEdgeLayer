# Positioning

## Category

MEL is a **local-first incident-intelligence + trusted-control OS** for mesh-adjacent operations.

## Who MEL is for first

1. Operators of local or mixed-channel networks who cannot tolerate fake health claims.
2. Teams that need post-incident evidence and replayable action history.
3. Environments where privacy/self-hosting are mandatory baseline requirements.

## What MEL does better than generic dashboards

- Distinguishes live vs stale vs partial vs imported/offline data.
- Preserves control-path governance (intent, approval, execution, audit).
- Produces support/proof artifacts directly from deterministic runtime data.
- Keeps “assistive intelligence” explicitly non-canonical.

## What MEL does not claim

- Mesh routing ownership.
- RF propagation certainty.
- BLE/HTTP ingest support.
- Unbounded enterprise collaboration features not present in runtime.

## Positioning tests (for external messaging)

A message is acceptable only if it passes all four:

- **Evidence test:** can we point to persisted records or deterministic checks?
- **Boundary test:** does it include unsupported/degraded caveats where relevant?
- **Operator test:** does it help a real shift operator decide safely?
- **Audit test:** could a skeptical reviewer verify the claim from exports/logs?
