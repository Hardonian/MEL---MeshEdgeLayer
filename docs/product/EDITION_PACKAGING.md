# Edition Packaging

This document defines packaging logic without overstating current implementation.

## Packaging principles

1. No paywall on core operator truth.
2. Commercial value comes from workflow scale, governance depth, and supportability.
3. Any paid claim must map to shipped capability or explicitly labeled near-term delivery.

## Proposed edition model

| Edition | Position | Scope now | Commercial posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Community (OSS Core) | Local-first truth + incident basics | Core ingest, timeline, diagnostics, baseline UI/API, basic exports | Open source |
| Pro Operator | Single-team operational acceleration | Operator workflow tooling, higher-value packaging around runbooks/support/readiness (as shipped) | Paid support/subscription |
| Team / Incident Intelligence | Multi-operator process maturity | Enhanced review workflows, deeper incident memory and governance artifacts as they ship | Commercial |
| Audit/Regulated (future) | Higher-trust evidence operations | Advanced audit workflows and policy controls, only when implemented | Roadmap only |

## Explicit non-claims

- No claim of enterprise tenancy isolation beyond currently implemented auth/capability model.
- No claim of compliance certifications unless independently completed.
- No claim of proprietary “AI autonomy.”

## Free vs paid framing

- **Free/OSS:** foundational local operation truth.
- **Paid:** operational leverage, governance depth, faster support outcomes, and packaging that reduces incident MTTR.
