# Differentiation and Moat

## Near-term differentiation

- Evidence-first operations model, not synthetic “healthy” badges.
- Explicit degraded/unknown semantics across ingest, control, and exports.
- Trusted-control lifecycle with durable action attribution and audit history.
- Proofpack/support-bundle workflows rooted in runtime evidence.

## Compounding moat vectors

1. **Incident memory density**: repeated failures become classifiable patterns.
2. **Adjudication memory**: operator decisions and outcomes create reusable policy and runbook intelligence.
3. **Evidence-chain quality**: deeper linkage between signals, actions, and outcomes raises trust and switching cost.
4. **Workflow lock-in by trust**: teams build investigative and support habits around MEL artifacts.

## What copied UI cannot replicate quickly

- Historical evidence quality and operator-decision corpus.
- Incident outcome memory tied to exact local topology/context.
- Auditable action governance records with bounded claim language.

## Guardrails

- Advisory intelligence remains advisory unless deterministic actuator proof exists.
- No claims stronger than evidence sufficiency.
- “Moat” is earned through accumulated outcomes, not branding language.
